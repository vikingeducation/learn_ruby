"Danebook" Project Kickoff

A Viking Software Consulting client with whom we've developed a relationship finally looks like they're ready to bring us on for a substantial project.

The Danish company -- called Midnight Sun Social -- is a small social company which previously produced a solution that helps teachers and students at Danish high schools to communicate when school is out of session. The product hasn't performed as well as they'd like, so they've got their eyes on something a bit different now.

Their team is impressed with the success of social products like Twitter, Google Plus and Facebook but their experience working with Danish high school students has given them some unique market insight. They believe that the future of social lies not in sprawling global networks but in tightly focused micronetworks.

In particular, they are interested in testing whether one of these micronetworks will appeal to their native Danes. Their research team has identified a latent need among Danes to connect exclusively with each other.

To test this hypothesis, their product team started mocking up some early designs and testing them with potential users. The early feedback was quite positive, so now they'd like to take the next steps.

Their previous product was developed by a low cost team thousands of miles away and they found the development process unnecessarily chaotic and inefficient. They felt like they spent so much time building detailed specs and debugging communication issues that they might as well have built the product themselves. It was particularly frustrating for them that the development team couldn't properly estimate their workload or stay on target to meet deadlines.

Midnight Sun Social came to Viking Software Consulting based on some recommendations from satisfied past clients whose projects were finished on time and on budget. The Midnight Sun Social leadership team has reached out to see if we'd be a good fit to build their product in the event that they decide to go forward with it.

As an early test of our ability and to help them validate their product, Midnight Sun Social would like us to create a set of static demo pages. They'll use these pages to further test their idea with users and to demo with potential investors in the project. If we work well together and the project gets traction, they're willing to take us on full time to build it.

The project's working title is "Danebook" and they've borrowed heavily from existing social networks for their prototypes.

Why This is Great for Viking Software Consulting

The Danebook project could be a great opportunity for us to build a serious project for a paying client while we're transitioning our team to using Rails on the back-end. It will be pretty much from scratch because we won't have access to our usual templates and libraries from the old stack but it'll give us a chance to produce some brand new ones using modern technology.

Another great aspect of the project is that they are fully willing to invest in the Agile Development process. They loved the ideas of constant iteration and communication between our two teams, especially in light of their poor experiences before.

The initial batch of work -- creating the static prototypes -- should be a good chance for us to ease into the Agile process and work with some of the new technologies we've recently learned. If the project goes forward, we should be able to re-use much of what we produce in this stage as well.

Though we've got a few other smaller projects in the pipeline, this one could be the big one we've been waiting for to prove that our transition to being an Agile Rails shop is a good one.
